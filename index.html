Jung's map of the mind

Carl Gustav Jung is a world renowned swiss psychologist and psychiatrist who invented analytical psychology. His work is so influential it’s close to impossible to not bump into it when looking into the field of psychology. It includes theories such as the collective unconscious where you can learn about the jungian archetypes such as the shadow, the persona, the trickster, the mother and the self. Dreams may possess universal meanings due to this shared part of the psyche. Freud's ice berg model

Sigmund Freud, the world famous neurologist and psychologist is the inventor of psychoanalysis and dream analysis. Though in newer forms, his creations are still used to this day even clinically. His theories include the well known id, ego, and superego also known and referred to as the devil, the mediator, and the angel. A quote often used by Freudian theorists goes as follows, “The mind is like an iceberg, it floats with one-seventh of its bulk above water.” emphasizing the depth of our minds. Read about Sigmund Freud to get a better understanding of the iceberg model and theories about dreams where personal symbolism is stressed.
